### Week 8: Molding, CNC, and Casting <br> <p style="color:rgb(48, 48, 255)"><font size="+2">For the eigth assignment, we were tasked with designing and making something that uses CNC and creating something by molding and casting. First, I decided to use the SRM-20 machine in order to make a 2.5D wax mold. My design was my three initials (A,C, and B) as well as a cool sword on top to add extra detail. Here is my 2.5D file that I created in Fusion360, starting with my sketch and then moving on to an extruded version of my design.</font></p> <style> .center { display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; width: 50%; } </style> <img src="./png1.png" width="400" height="242" alt="image1" class="center"> <br> <style> .center { display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; width: 50%; } </style> <img src="./png2.png" width="400" height="242" alt="image2" class="center"> <br> <p style="color:rgb(48, 48, 255)"><font size="+2">After extruding the STL file, I put it on a USB flash drive and put it on the computer that was connected to the SRM-20 machine. I applied double-sided tape to the bottom of the wax block that I chose, and I set it down in the machine. However, I ran into a problem after my first calibration and running of the machine: The dimensions of the file were incorrect. After readjusting, I put the wax block back into the SRM-20 and let it do one rough pass (as I do not need a fine pass for my simple design).
